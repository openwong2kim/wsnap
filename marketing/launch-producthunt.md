## Description (≤260 chars)
Press a key, drag a region — the screenshot is instantly on your clipboard, and the floating
thumbnail drags out as a real file. Dark annotation editor, pixel loupe, offline KO/EN OCR,
GIF, scrolling capture. Free, private, open source.

## First comment (maker's comment)

Hi PH 👋 I'm the maker of wsnap.

I built it because Windows capture tools kept getting two everyday things wrong: I'd have to
hunt for the saved file, or they'd copy the *file path* when I wanted the *image*. wsnap makes
both first-class:

🖼️ Capture → the image is **on your clipboard instantly** (paste anywhere).
🪄 The floating thumbnail is a **real file you drag** into Slack/editors/email — and it stays,
so you can drop it in several places.
✏️ A toolbar at your selection: copy, save, edit, OCR, GIF, pin.
🔤 **Offline OCR that's actually good on Korean + English** (PaddleOCR PP-OCRv5 on ONNX,
models embedded — no language pack). Windows' built-in OCR kept mangling mixed text for me.
🌙 One dark UI across overlay/editor/settings, pixel loupe + HEX readout, mixed-DPI correct.
🔒 No account, no forced uploads, opt-in telemetry, local log. **Open source, GPL-3.0.**

It's a single self-contained exe (portable zip + Scoop too), lean in the tray. Honest caveats:
Windows x64 only, and it's **not code-signed yet** so SmartScreen will warn (signing is in
progress).

Would love your feedback — especially on the capture → drag-out flow, and on OCR quality if
you work with non-Latin scripts. Thanks for checking it out!
